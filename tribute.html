Kalpana Chawla

Kalpana Chawla was born on March 17, 1962, in Karnal, India. She earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from Punjab Engineering College before moving to the United States to pursue her dream of becoming an astronaut.

Chawla joined NASA in 1995 and flew her first space mission in 1997 aboard the Space Shuttle Columbia. She went on to fly a second mission in 2003, but tragically, the shuttle disintegrated upon re-entry, killing all seven crew members, including Chawla.

Despite her untimely death, Kalpana Chawla's legacy lives on as a trailblazer for women in STEM and an inspiration to all those who dream of exploring the unknown.
